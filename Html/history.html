History

K2 - The Savage Mountain

Height: 8,611 meters (28,251 feet)
Location: Pakistan-China Border (Karakoram Range)
First Ascent: 31 July 1954, by an Italian Expedition
Difficulty Level: Extremely Challenging

Interesting Facts:

- Known as the "Savage Mountain" due to its extreme difficulty.
- One of the deadliest peaks, with a high fatality rate among climbers.
- K2 remains unclimbed in winter until 2021, when a Nepali team made history.

Mount Everest - The Roof of the World

Height: 8,849 meters (29,032 feet)
Location: Nepal-Tibet Border (Himalayas)
First Ascent: 29 May 1953, by Sir Edmund Hillary & Tenzing Norgay
Difficulty Level: Extremely Challenging

Interesting Facts:

- Mount Everest is the **highest mountain in the world**.
- The peak grows **about 4mm every year** due to shifting tectonic plates.
- The "Death Zone" (above 8,000m) has **thin oxygen levels**, making survival difficult.
- More than **300 climbers** have lost their lives attempting to reach the summit.
